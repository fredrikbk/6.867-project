credit crunch china citic denies bid for bear stearns stake shanghai china citic bank corp said it hasnt talked with bear stearns cos about buying a stake in the u s investment firm and wont in the near term "so far the company doesnt have any plan to buy a stake in bear stearns and the company promised it wont plan such an event in the next three months " citic bank wrote in a release to investors on the shanghai stock exchange where it is listed the statement applies only to the bank not other companies within the massive citic group a diversified financial conglomerate based in beijing this week china banking regulatory commission vice chairman jiang dingzhi said citic bank had "bid" for an investment in bear stearns mr jiang made his statement during a session of a communist party conclave in beijing as part of a rundown of recent chinese banking industry activities the venue suggested mr jiang was speaking about past rather than current activity but the comment underscored a view that chinese investors are interested in making wall street investments a citic group vice chairman chang zhenming who is known to have talked about an investment and banking deal with bear stearns last year when he worked for another chinese bank this week confirmed his business group had expressed an interest in the u s investment bank at one time but he said there hasnt been any "material progress " the banks statement yesterday was made in accordance with chinese securities regulations that require disclosure of major deals
