new tool devised to digitize books associated press researchers at carnegie mellon university have discovered a way to enlist people to help digitize books every time they solve the simple distorted word puzzles commonly used to ensure that a web site visitor is a real person and not an automated program computers cant decipher the twisted letters although it takes people just seconds on average to type them in researchers estimate that about million of these puzzles are solved everyday around the world now carnegie mellon researchers have come up with a way for the puzzles to give snippets of books instead of random words to type in that could help get searchable texts online faster the idea is that images not recognized by optical systems can be used in the puzzles at web sites around the internet
