qualcomm inc deal ends two disputes with rival broadcom qualcomm inc and broadcom corp agreed to end two patent disputes but the chip makers still have four other lawsuits set to go to trial this year the companies agreed to dismiss all claims and counterclaims associated with two patents from each side relating to bluetooth technology in cellphones terms of the agreement werent disclosed the remaining patent infringement claims between the parties which will be reviewed by federal courts in san diego and orange county calif arent affected by the deal qualcomm san diego holds many patents for a popular cellular technology known as cdma or code division multiple access and dominates sales of chips needed to make cdma phones the company licenses patents to cellphone makers and other chip companies broadcom an irvine calif maker of communications chips is a relative newcomer to cellphones it has unsuccessfully negotiated for a patent license from qualcomm which it accuses of demanding unreasonable terms
