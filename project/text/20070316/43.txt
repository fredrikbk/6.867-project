former livedoor chief is sentenced tokyo japanese entrepreneur takafumi horie was sentenced to two and a half years in jail on friday for violating securities laws ending a headline grabbing trial that has highlighted the tensions japan faces as it strives to improve the transparency of its capital markets the year old internet entrepreneur was accused of manipulating the earnings of his company livedoor co through a series of stock splits and by buying shares via "dummy" companies though the sentence is lighter than the four years prosecutors sought it is harsher than many handed down in business trials executives are often found guilty but given suspended sentences mr horie is expected to appeal the ruling mr hories conviction comes as regulators move to a more transparent system of governing business transactions in general and the capital markets in particular the change comes after decades of so called "administrative guidance" in which regulators enforced rules on a case by case basis with little public disclosure
