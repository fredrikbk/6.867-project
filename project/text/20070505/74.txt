student loan inquiry reviews federal workers washington the inspector general for the u s department of education is examining whether federal employees acted unethically or breached rules when they established business ties with lending companies and others involved in federal student loan programs the inspector john p higgins jr said in a letter to u s rep george miller chairman of the house committee on education and labor that the inquiry could involve examining financial disclosure forms and ethics files of as many as individuals the investigation follows news reports that a financial aid official at the u s department of education held shares in the parent of lender student loan xpress inc that official is on leave pending a review congress and several attorneys general began inquiries into the links between colleges public officials and student loan and consolidation firms after new york attorney general andrew cuomo found that several college officers had accepted gifts and payments from lenders and that some alumni associations accepted payments from at least one loan consolidator in exchange for recommending their services
