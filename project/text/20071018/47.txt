eu orders fee cancellations for french bank cards european union regulators ordered french banks to drop plans to introduce credit and debit card fees saying this stacked the deck against smaller banks and kept prices artificially high and customer choice too narrow the european commission the eus executive arm told groupement des cartes bancaires which manages "cb" card payments that include visa and mastercard to immediately cancel a set of charges to be paid by all member banks that discouraged new rival banks from issuing more cards at lower rates more than of card payments in france are made in the "cb" system run by big french banks
