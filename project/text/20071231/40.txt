japans fukuda visits china associated press beijing japanese prime minister yasuo fukuda returned home sunday from china after a symbolically important visit that indicated warming relations between asias most powerful nations the four day trip was mr fukudas first to china since he became prime minister in september and was the latest sign of improved ties between the countries which often squabble over territory energy resources and wartime history mr fukuda visited the birthplace of the philosopher confucius before heading home the stop in the northeastern city of qufu demonstrated "the common cultural background between the chinese people and japanese people " said spokesman mitsuo sakaba of japans foreign ministry the teachings of confucius influenced both countries cultures in ancient times visiting the temple of confucius mr fukuda stopped to write chinese calligraphy on a scroll "reviewing the past to create something new " mr fukudas visit builds on warming ties after his predecessor shinzo abe visited china in october
