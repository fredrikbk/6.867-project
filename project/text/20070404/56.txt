hong kong signs pact with china hong kong securities regulators in hong kong and china have reached an agreement to enhance cross border enforcement giving the citys securities watchdog more authority to gather evidence against any listed chinese companies that breach local regulations most new listings in hong kong are by chinese companies which account for almost half of the stock exchanges total market value market observers said the deal is a small step in improving the corporate governance of hong kong listed chinese firms and more needs to be done on the part of regulators to enforce compliance the new arrangement signed between hong kongs securities and futures commission and the china securities regulatory commission takes immediate effect
