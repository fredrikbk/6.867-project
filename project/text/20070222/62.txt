in japan merger rejection breaks tradition tokyo an activist fund successfully rallied shareholders in tokyo kohtetsu co a small japanese steel company to reject a proposed merger a major change in the countrys burgeoning movement toward stronger corporate governance investment fund ichigo asset management ltd led by a former morgan stanley managing director secured one third of the companys vote giving it the power to veto an integration planned with osaka steel co the victory marks the first time shareholders in a japanese company have blocked a merger that was approved by the management of both companies ichigo which opened a proxy solicitation earlier this month has said the deal makes sense for the two companies but has argued that the price offered by osaka steel was too low the offer initially had a small premium but that eroded throughout the fight the victory comes as japan increasingly adopts a more shareholder friendly form of capitalism that is championing the owners of companies it is a break from traditional japanese business practices that put the interests of employees and business partners first
