moving the market india unveils derivatives guidelines new delhi bankers responded positively to draft guidelines setting out rules for the development of credit derivatives in india the countrys next step toward widening its financial markets bankers said they liked the depth and breadth of the proposed guidelines issued by the central bank and that they offered a good framework to eventually develop an indian derivatives market starting with credit default swaps the reserve bank of india in a document posted on its web site late wednesday has given commercial banks and other financial institutions a month to study and comment on the proposed rules "these are draft guidelines put out for public comment and suggestions " a spokeswoman for the central bank said credit derivatives allow banks and other lenders to hedge against loan defaults by paying investors to assume their credit risk with indian bank lending rapidly rising and the government considering borrowing from private institutions to fund its huge infrastructure needs the transfer of credit risk could help indian banks better manage and spread the risk they carry on their books
