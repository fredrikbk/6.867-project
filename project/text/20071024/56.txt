house bill seeks to protect online identities washington a house of representatives panel approved legislation that would make it illegal for u s technology companies to pass customers information to governments seeking to restrict access to the internet the global online freedom act was unanimously approved by the house foreign affairs committee it will penalize companies that assist "repressive regimes" that use personal information to track down democracy activists "dictatorships need two pillars to survive propaganda and secret police " said rep chris smith d n j the author of the legislation "the internet if misused gives them both in spades " the legislation comes as the panel continues to seek answers from yahoo inc about its alleged role in the arrest of chinese journalist shi tao by authorities in china a spokesperson for yahoo didnt respond to an email seeking comment for this article
