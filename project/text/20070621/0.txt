carried interest change in tax rules is senseless holman jenkinss june business world column "this years man behind the tree " concerning congresss desire to tax "carried interest" profits at a higher rate was informative but it missed a subtle point carried interest earned by private equity funds is nothing other than a slice of the profits earned by a funds limited partners as such carried interest is currently taxed at whatever tax rate is applicable to those profits for example carried interest is usually taxed at the lower capital gains rate if the profits arise from the sale of a business held for more than one year if the profits are from more short term ventures then tax rates for ordinary income usually apply changing the rules so that carried interest would always be taxed at higher ordinary tax rates makes no sense this would be analogous to changing the tax rate on all stock sales to the ordinary tax rate even if the investors holding period exceeded one year john fox san rafael calif
