judge extends bar on illegal worker crackdown a federal judge in san francisco extended for days a temporary restraining order blocking the government from implementing a program to crack down on employers of illegal immigrants the case revolves around the bush administrations plan for the social security administration to send out thousands of "no match" letters to employers whose workers names dont match their social security numbers the notices would be accompanied by letters from the department of homeland security outlining penalties for hiring undocumented workers on aug the court barred the government from launching the workplace initiative after a lawsuit by the afl cio charged that it would hurt u s citizens and foreign workers later several trade groups representing small businesses joined the suit to halt the government program alleging that the government didnt assess the financial impact on small companies the judge is expected to rule on the case within the days "we remain optimistic that the judge will rule favorably upon our position " said a spokesman for the department of homeland security
