energy independence creates new addiction daniel yergins "energy independence" editorial page jan debunks various myths about energy such as the perception that most of our energy comes from the middle east or that buying oil is tantamount to funding terrorism such myths have made "energy independence" a mantra in the press many of the proposed "solutions" arbitrarily high taxes on fossil fuels tariffs massive government subsidies for whichever alternative fuel programs gain political support are far worse than the actual problem they reek of protectionism which if imitated abroad could trigger global recession i would add to mr yergins thoughtful column that most "energy independence" schemes simply create a new type of dependence on expensive subsidies taxation regulations and on the politicians lobbyists and bureaucrats who would devise and enforce these questionable programs robinson nottingham washington
