eu plans to simplify vat rates brussels the european commission launched a debate to find ways to simplify varying value added tax rates on goods and services across the nation european union eu tax commissioner laszlo kovacs told a news conference he wants a new regime in place by at the latest eu countries enjoy exemptions from the blocs sales tax rate on a range of goods and services from childrens clothes and restaurant meals to hairdressing and window cleaning this has led to a confusing patchwork of regimes that can turn into an administrative headache for businesses trying to operate across borders the commission said one solution would be to allow for a very low rate on necessities such as food and a second slightly higher rate on goods and services requiring preferential treatment for cultural educational or environmental reasons
