guidelines detect deadly meningitis reuters news service doctors can follow a set of guidelines to identify a deadly form of meningitis in children sparing young patients days in the hospital researchers said meningitis produces swelling of the membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord the bacterial form that occurs in one in cases can produce illness more severe than that caused by the viral form doctors often cannot determine which type of meningitis a patient has until a bacterial culture is produced in the laboratory which can require a wait as long as three days as a precaution most patients are admitted to hospitals and treated with powerful antibiotics however guidelines developed four years ago and recently tested in a trial of patients aged days to years correctly classified all but two patients both of whom were younger than two months and therefore more difficult to diagnose results of the study were published in this weeks issue of the journal of the american medical association the criteria include routine tests of blood and fluids and a checklist that includes whether the child has had seizures since falling ill "the ability to identify those children who are at very low risk of bacterial meningitis and can be considered for management on an outpatient basis will avoid unnecessary hospitalization and aggressive antibiotic therapy " said study author nathan kuppermann of the university of california davis medical center a vaccine to combat bacterial meningitis has been recommended in the united states for at risk children younger than two since reducing the incidence of the disease the report said "the finding is important because it gives emergency room physicians nationwide a tool to guide their decision making when caring for children with suspected meningitis a serious and potentially life threatening infection " said study co author lise nigrovic of childrens hospital boston
