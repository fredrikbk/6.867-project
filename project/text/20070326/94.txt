total ceo says he wont resign over charges the chief executive of total sa has no plans to resign despite an investigating judge filing preliminary corruption charges against him over a contract with iran the oil company said friday a resignation by christophe de margerie who took over as ceo last month "is absolutely not on the agenda " said total spokeswoman catherine enck she reiterated the companys denial of any wrongdoing in the deal to develop the south pars gas field in the persian gulf an investigating judge filed preliminary charges thursday against mr de margerie for alleged abuse of company assets and corruption of foreign officials judicial officials said under french law preliminary charges mean the investigating magistrate has determined there is strong evidence to suggest involvement in a crime that gives the magistrate time to pursue a deeper probe the magistrate can then send the individual for trial or conclude that no crime was committed and drop the investigation
