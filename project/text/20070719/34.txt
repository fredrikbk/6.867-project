fund manager receives sentence tokyo japanese fund manager yoshiaki murakami was sentenced to two years in prison for using insider information during a takeover battle the tokyo district court found that mr murakami years old had purchased shares in radio network nippon broadcasting system inc with knowledge that an internet conglomerate was planning a takeover attempt of the company the sentence was lighter than the three years prosecutors had called for but still unusual in a country where white collar criminals rarely see prison time the sentencing comes as japan wrestles with clarifying rules for mergers and acquisitions private equity buyouts and broader corporate governance as tokyo endeavors to boost its role as a financial center in asia
