manufacturing shows no long term decline the graphic accompanying the jan "outlook" column "for some manufacturers there are benefits to keeping production at home" that shows a declining manufacturing share of gross domestic product since is technically correct but mainly the result of a decline in the price of manufactured goods relative to other goods and services when manufacturing is expressed in real terms its share of gdp has remained about the same as in it is indeed counter cyclical declining in recessions and increasing in expansions but has shown no long term decrease while it is true that the fraction of employment in manufacturing has declined substantially because of large productivity gains in these industries there is little evidence that america has "de industrialized " michael bond ph d finance department cleveland state university cleveland
