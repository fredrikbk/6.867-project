making excuses for protectionists peter mandelson makes a number of misleading claims in his feb letter he claims eu farm tariff cuts will do little for people in the developing world because of the erosion of existing trade preferences but research by the world bank shows that even after preference erosion is taken into account there would still be large gains for developing countries from making large cuts in agricultural tariffs in the doha round other world bank research found in contrast to mr mandelsons claim that the overwhelming majority of the gains for developing countries from the end of all tariffs and subsidies would come from tariff rather than subsidy cuts in fact tariff cuts could provide up to of the benefits on the other hand the huge number of "sensitive" products the eu plans to exempt from full cuts really would slash the benefits for poor countries peter mandelson must stop making excuses for protectionist member states and start making more ambitious offers neil obrien director open europe london
